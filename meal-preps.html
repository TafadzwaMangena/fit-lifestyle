7 days of healthy meals on a budget


Eating well is an important part of a healthy lifestyle. Consuming a balanced mix of nutritious foods can help your body feel its best. Aim for a mix of whole fruits and veggies, grains, proteins, fats, and oils from foods you enjoy.


Planning your meals in advance can help you to make healthier choices, save money and reduce waste. We’ve planned a week of meals that will give you a balanced, heart-healthy diet whilst helping you to control your spending. Amounts given are per person.

8 Healthy and Effective Diets
Day Breatfast Lunch Evening Meal Snacks
Monday Porridge made with skimmed milk and one sliced banana on top. Lentil and vegetable soup, tinned or try our easy carrot and parsnip soup recipe (pictured); two slices of wholemeal bread with spread (spreads based on vegetable, olive or sunflower oil will be unsaturated so a better choice for your heart than butter). One medium-sized jacket potato (cooked in the microwave or oven) with a tin of sardines in tomato sauce, served with peas. Two satsumas (or easy-peelers); one apple; a small handful of unsalted peanuts (30g); a serving (125g) of fat-free Greek-style yoghurt.
Tuesday Two slices of wholegrain toast with spread, one boiled egg and a sliced medium tomato. Our carrot and parsnip soup (use leftovers if you made Monday’s recipe, or make fresh with extra portions to freeze); two slices of wholemeal bread with spread. Homemade chunky vegetable and bean goulash (pictured); brown rice; broccoli. Get our goulash recipe. Three oatcakes with low-fat cream cheese or quark; one pear.
Wednesday No-added-sugar muesli with skimmed milk, topped with one sliced banana. Jacket potato with half a can of reduced-sugar-and-salt baked beans; a portion of salad. Beetroot barley risotto (pictured); served with peas. One carrot cut into sticks; a small handful of unsalted peanuts; two satsumas.
Thursday Two slices of wholegrain toast with spread, sliced or mashed banana, and a serving of fat-free Greek-style yoghurt. Egg, tomato and cucumber wholemeal bread sandwich. Wholewheat spaghetti with sardines and cherry tomatoes; a portion of salad. Two plums; two oatcakes with spread; one pear.
Friday No-added-sugar muesli with skimmed milk and a banana. Cheese salad sandwich made with reduced-fat Cheddar, salad, two slices of wholegrain bread and spread. Homemade mushroom and cauliflower frittata, served with peas and carrots or any other leftover/surplus vegetables. Get our frittata recipe. Two plums; two oatcakes with spread; one pear.
Saturday Porridge made with skimmed milk; one banana. Vegetable soup, tinned or try our delicious recipe for mixed vegetable and bean soup. Homemade tuna and sweetcorn pasta bake, served with cauliflower and broccoli. Small handful of unsalted peanuts, one apple, one pear..
Sunday Poached egg and a portion of cooked mushrooms, with two slices of wholemeal bread. Chicken and vegetable traybake (pictured); baked apple with spiced sultanas and low-fat custard or homemade custard with low-fat milk. Chicken, cucumber and tomato wholemeal bread sandwiches (using leftovers from the lunchtime traybake). Small handful of unsalted peanuts; one carrot cut into sticks; two satsumas (or easy-peelers).
